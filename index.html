About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Linguistics at the University of Texas at Austin. My research focuses on the documentation and description of underrepresented Quechua varieties in Bolivia, with particular emphasis on TAME categories. My work combines formal linguistic analysis with community-based field research, exploring questions of how speakers encode and handle knowledge in discourse.

I am originally from a Quechua-speaking community in Bolivia, I completed my undergraduate studies in Linguistics, where I developed a strong interest in, language description, documentation and teaching. I later taught English, French, and Spanish in Bolivia and France. These experiences broadened my comparative perspective and my understanding of multilingualism, language description, pedagogy, and cultural preservation.

My broader research interests include language description, endangerment, revitalization strategies. In addition to my academic work, I am the author of a collection of traditional stories entitled “Iphu” published in 2020 and a book on plant-based medicine collection, both centered on the preservation of inherited knowledge and oral traditions in Andean Rural Quechua communities.
